Process

Process

1
Research

Before going into creative development research is conducted to better understand the project. Research is divided into two sections: literary research—compiling information about the client and target audience—and visual research—drawing inspiration from other design solutions.

3
Develop

The numerous solutions created in the Ideate step are then narrowed down to a single direction, choosing the option that best achieves the project’s objectives. At this stage, decisions about colors, typography, layouts, etc. are made, visualizing how the final result could look.

2
Ideate

The ideation process begins with a “brain dump,” where the problem is tackled from various perspectives. This typically takes the form of mind maps and rough thumbnail sketches, exploring a diverse range of solutions and always referencing the research that was conducted.

4
Revise

Once a testable prototype is developed, it goes through a series of checkpoints, receiving feedback from the client as well as user groups. The design is refined based on feedback and testing results and finalized until it meets the project’s objectives and satisfies the client’s needs.
